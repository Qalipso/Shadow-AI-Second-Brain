# Shadow — Competitive Landscape

## 1. Positioning Map

Shadow sits between four product categories:

1. AI Second Brain
2. Planning / Calendar Automation
3. Life Analytics / Quantified Self
4. Passive Memory / Lifelogging

Shadow should not copy one category directly.

The opportunity is to combine:
- low-friction capture
- AI auto-structure
- guided reflection
- 12-area life analytics
- RAG memory
- daily/weekly reports
- personal operating system logic

---

## 2. Category 1 — AI Second Brain

## 2.1 Mem

### What it does
Mem focuses on AI-powered note capture, organization, and retrieval. It helps users get thoughts out of their head and find them later.

### Strengths
- low-friction capture
- AI organization
- personal knowledge retrieval
- good second-brain positioning

### Weaknesses / Gap
- mostly note/memory oriented
- not a full life analytics system
- less focused on daily questions, life areas, food, money, emotions, and reports

### Shadow learning
Shadow should copy the low-friction brain-dump idea, but go further into structured life analytics.

---

## 2.2 Reflect

### What it does
Reflect is an AI note-taking tool using AI to improve writing, organize thoughts, and act as an intellectual thought partner.

### Strengths
- clean writing experience
- AI-assisted thinking
- note quality
- good reflective UX

### Weaknesses / Gap
- more focused on notes than full life operations
- not centered around 12-area life analytics
- less focused on structured extraction of tasks, food, expenses, and mood

### Shadow learning
Shadow should maintain a calm, reflective feel while adding structured data and dashboard intelligence.

---

## 2.3 Capacities

### What it does
Capacities organizes notes as connected objects instead of files and folders.

### Strengths
- object-based knowledge model
- better fit for human thinking than folders
- strong personal knowledge architecture

### Weaknesses / Gap
- still requires user commitment and understanding of structure
- can feel complex
- not primarily an AI life dashboard

### Shadow learning
Shadow should use object-like entities internally, but hide complexity from the user through AI auto-classification.

---

## 2.4 Tana

### What it does
Tana combines knowledge graph concepts, AI, structured notes, and now agentic meeting workflows.

### Strengths
- graph-based thinking
- powerful structured data
- AI agents
- organizational memory

### Weaknesses / Gap
- can be complex for casual users
- increasingly work/meeting oriented
- not focused on personal life analytics

### Shadow learning
Shadow can borrow the graph/memory mindset, but should stay simpler and more personal.

---

## 2.5 Notion AI

### What it does
Notion is an AI workspace for docs, projects, knowledge, search, automation, and agents.

### Strengths
- flexible workspace
- powerful databases
- team and individual use
- AI features
- strong ecosystem

### Weaknesses / Gap
- requires manual setup
- can become overloaded
- user must design their own system
- not optimized for automatic life analytics

### Shadow learning
Shadow should avoid becoming a blank workspace. It should provide opinionated structure out of the box.

---

## 3. Category 2 — Planning / Calendar Automation

## 3.1 Motion

### What it does
Motion focuses on AI task planning, AI calendar assistance, AI project management, and automatic scheduling.

### Strengths
- strong execution layer
- automatic daily planning
- task prioritization
- calendar integration

### Weaknesses / Gap
- work/productivity oriented
- less focused on emotions, food, personal reflection, meaning, or memory
- not a life-wide analytical mirror

### Shadow learning
Shadow may later integrate scheduling, but MVP should focus on understanding and structuring life first.

---

## 3.2 Reclaim

### What it does
Reclaim automatically schedules tasks, habits, meetings, breaks, and focus time into calendars.

### Strengths
- protects time
- strong calendar automation
- habits as scheduled blocks
- practical for work/life calendar management

### Weaknesses / Gap
- calendar-first
- not memory-first
- not focused on personal meaning, reflection, or life analytics

### Shadow learning
Shadow can later use calendar automation, but should first build a memory and analytics foundation.

---

## 3.3 Sunsama

### What it does
Sunsama is a calm daily planner with guided planning and weekly reviews.

### Strengths
- excellent planning ritual
- calm UX
- daily and weekly reflection
- workload awareness

### Weaknesses / Gap
- more manual
- not primarily AI auto-structured
- not built around long-term memory graph or life-wide categories

### Shadow learning
Shadow should learn from Sunsama’s calm planning ritual and apply it to AI-guided life review.

---

## 3.4 Akiflow

### What it does
Akiflow is a universal inbox, task manager, and calendar planning tool.

### Strengths
- universal task capture
- calendar/time-blocking
- fast task workflow

### Weaknesses / Gap
- task-centric
- not focused on emotional, behavioral, food, finance, and meaning analytics

### Shadow learning
Shadow’s AI Inbox should feel as fast as a task inbox, but accept broader life input.

---

## 4. Category 3 — Life Analytics / Quantified Self

## 4.1 Exist

### What it does
Exist helps users track mood, behavior, habits, and correlations to understand what affects their days.

### Strengths
- personal analytics
- correlations
- mood tracking
- behavior insight

### Weaknesses / Gap
- less focused on AI capture
- less focused on tasks/goals/projects
- not a full personal operating system

### Shadow learning
Shadow should borrow the idea of correlations and personal analytics, but make capture easier through AI.

---

## 4.2 Bearable

### What it does
Bearable tracks symptoms, mood, pain, fatigue, sleep, energy, habits, and health-related factors.

### Strengths
- strong tracking depth
- useful for health pattern discovery
- good correlation logic
- structured reports

### Weaknesses / Gap
- health-first
- may feel medical/tracker-heavy
- not focused on work, creativity, goals, meaning, and life operating system

### Shadow learning
Shadow should support health/body signals, but avoid becoming a medical product.

---

## 4.3 Daylio

### What it does
Daylio is a mood diary and activity tracker with charts, statistics, goals, and reports.

### Strengths
- simple daily input
- low friction
- good mood/activity statistics
- approachable UX

### Weaknesses / Gap
- limited depth
- less AI-driven
- less useful for tasks, goals, memory, and complex life structure

### Shadow learning
Shadow should learn from Daylio’s simplicity. Daily input must stay easy.

---

## 5. Category 4 — Passive Memory / Lifelogging

## 5.1 Limitless / Rewind

### What it does
Limitless is positioned as personalized AI powered by what the user has seen, said, and heard.

### Strengths
- powerful memory promise
- passive capture
- searchable conversations
- personal AI context

### Weaknesses / Gap
- privacy concerns
- passive capture may feel invasive
- memory does not automatically equal structured life direction
- not centered around daily questions and life analytics

### Shadow learning
Shadow should use memory, but remain intentional, user-controlled, and structured.

---

## 6. Shadow Differentiation

Shadow is different because it combines:

- natural language life capture
- guided daily questions
- AI auto-classification
- structured life database
- 12-area Life Circle
- RAG memory
- daily and weekly reports
- cognitive load reduction
- personal dogfooding
- portfolio-ready product architecture

Shadow is not trying to replace:
- Notion as a workspace
- Motion as a calendar
- Daylio as a simple mood tracker
- Limitless as passive recording
- Capacities as a PKM system

Shadow’s wedge:

“Turn chaotic daily life input into structured self-knowledge, memory, analytics, and action.”

## 8. Product Wedge

The first wedge should not be “all-in-one life app.”

Better wedge:

“An AI dashboard that helps overloaded builders turn daily chaos into structure and weekly clarity.”

This is specific enough for MVP and broad enough for future expansion.

---

## 9. Strategic Position

Short positioning:
Shadow is an AI life analytics platform for people with too much in their head.

Long positioning:
Shadow helps overloaded builders capture messy thoughts, tasks, emotions, goals, and daily events through one AI interface, then automatically structures them into a personal life database, 12-area dashboard, memory graph, and daily/weekly reports.

---

## 10. MVP Differentiators

MVP should clearly demonstrate:

1. AI understands messy life input.
2. User does not manually sort data.
3. Life Circle gives immediate structure.
4. Daily questions solve blank-page problem.
5. Reports are grounded in actual entries.
6. Memory connects today with previous patterns.
7. Dashboard gives clarity quickly.

If these seven points work, Shadow has a strong product story.
