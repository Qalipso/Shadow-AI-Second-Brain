Shadow — Product Visual Inventory
1. Purpose of this document

This document defines which product visuals appear on the landing page, what each visual proves, how detailed it should be and whether it is based on the current product or forward-looking UI.

The goal is to prevent the landing from showing vague futuristic dashboards that do not match the real product. Every visual should support a clear product claim.

2. Visual strategy

The landing should mix three types of visuals:

Realistic product UI fragments that prove Shadow is functional.
Stylized cinematic UI moments that explain complex ideas like memory, modes and Life Circle.
Character-driven Shady scenes that make the product emotionally memorable.

The product should not look like a random AI dashboard. Every UI fragment should be connected to one of these product ideas: capture, structure, memory, modes, analytics, reflection, labs, trust.

3. Source of truth rule

Each visual must be labeled as one of three fidelity types.

Real Product UI: based on screens that exist or will exist directly in the app.

Stylized Product Mockup: based on real product logic but visually adapted for the landing page.

Conceptual Illustration: used to explain the product metaphor rather than show exact interface.

The landing can use stylized mockups, but it should not imply that conceptual visuals are exact screenshots if they are not.

7. Product visual risks

If visuals are too abstract, users will not understand what Shadow does.

If visuals are too dashboard-heavy, Shadow will feel like generic AI analytics.

If visuals are too mystical, Shadow will lose product credibility.

If visuals are too literal, the landing will lose cinematic power.

The solution is to make every visual both product-relevant and Shady-driven.
