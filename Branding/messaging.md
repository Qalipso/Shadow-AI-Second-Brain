Shadow — Messaging & Copy Document
1. Purpose of this document

This document defines the voice, copy system, page messaging, terminology and conversion language for the Shadow landing page. It prevents the landing from becoming a beautiful visual shell with placeholder marketing language. Shadow depends heavily on copy because the product category is new, emotionally sensitive and conceptually deep.

The copy must explain Shadow without flattening it into a generic AI productivity tool. It must feel premium, calm and precise while still being emotionally memorable.

2. Primary copy objective

The copy should make the user understand and feel one core idea:

Shadow helps you stop holding your whole life in your head.

Everything else supports this: memory, modes, Shady, Life Circle, Labs, daily signals, AI questions and analytics.

3. Voice definition

Shadow speaks like a quiet intelligence that notices what matters.

The voice is:

calm
precise
intelligent
slightly mystical
emotionally aware
minimal
premium
direct
non-clinical
non-corporate
non-childish

Shadow does not shout. Shadow does not hype. Shadow does not diagnose. Shadow does not motivate with empty productivity slogans. Shadow helps the user see clearly.

4. Tone principles

Use short, strong sentences.

Make abstract ideas feel concrete.

Use emotional language carefully, without becoming therapeutic.

Use product language enough to prove this is a real system.

Balance poetry with utility.

Avoid exaggerated AI claims.

Avoid generic SaaS language.

Avoid diagnosis, treatment, clinical promises or mental health authority.

Avoid implying that the product knows the user better than they know themselves.

Prefer “Shadow helps you see” over “Shadow tells you who you are.”

Prefer “signals, memory, patterns, modes, clarity” over “optimization, productivity, performance, hacks.”

7. Core copy hierarchy
Level 1 — Main promise

Stop holding your whole life in your head.

Level 2 — Product explanation

Shadow captures your thoughts, tasks, emotions and daily signals, then turns them into living memory, patterns and clear next actions.

Level 3 — Mechanism

Dump anything into Shadow. It classifies the signal, asks what is missing, stores the context and connects it to your life map.

Level 4 — Emotional payoff

Less mental noise. More continuity. A clearer relationship with your own patterns.

11. Glossary

Shadow: the product and full personal operating system.

Shady: the living shadow mascot and emotional interface.

Signal: any raw input from life, including a thought, task, emotion, event, habit, goal, decision or observation.

Memory Layer: the system that stores and connects signals over time.

Living Memory: the user-facing emotional name for the memory experience.

Memory Graph: the visual representation of connected memory nodes.

Life Circle: the life analytics wheel showing balance and state across major life spheres.

Modes: state-based interfaces for different user needs.

Capture Mode: fast cognitive offload.

Focus Mode: prioritization and next action.

Reflection Mode: emotional and experiential processing.

Planning Mode: goals, projects and initiatives.

Recovery Mode: decompression and reduced cognitive pressure.

Deep Analysis Mode: pattern recognition and deeper insight.

Labs: experimental self-discovery, diagnostics and archetype experiences.

Insight: a meaningful conclusion generated from signals and memory.

Pattern: a repeated relationship detected across time.

Daily Sync: lightweight daily check-in.

Weekly Reflection: weekly summary and pattern review.

Initiative: a larger goal or project that connects actions over time.

12. Multilingual decision

Recommended primary landing language: English.

Reason: Shadow is a portfolio-grade AI product and may be positioned toward a global startup/product audience. English gives the product more SaaS credibility and better portfolio leverage.

Recommended secondary language: Russian for internal docs, founder content, product thinking posts and future localized landing.

Important rule: do not write the Russian version first and translate it directly into English. English Shadow should be written natively or by a bilingual copywriter. Direct translation will make the premium tone feel heavy and unnatural.

English voice should be minimal, precise and atmospheric.

Russian voice can be slightly more philosophical and expressive, but still restrained.

13. Copy guardrails

Do not promise diagnosis.

Do not promise mental health treatment.

Do not say Shadow knows the true user.

Do not say the product automatically fixes life.

Do not make productivity the only benefit.

Do not overuse “AI-powered.”

Do not make Shady too cute.

Do not use corporate phrases that could belong to any SaaS product.

Do not use mystical language without product grounding.

Every poetic claim should be supported by a product mechanism.
